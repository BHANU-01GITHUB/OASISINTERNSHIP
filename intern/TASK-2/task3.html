Bhagat Singh

1907 - 1931

Biography

Bhagat Singh (28 September 1907 - 23 March 1931) was a prominent Indian freedom fighter and revolutionary. He played a crucial role in the Indian independence movement against British colonial rule. Bhagat Singh's dedication to the cause of freedom made him a symbol of courage and sacrifice.

Known for his acts of protest against the oppressive British regime, Bhagat Singh, along with his associates, sought retribution for the brutal death of Lala Lajpat Rai during a demonstration. The Lahore Conspiracy Case and subsequent hunger strikes showcased his unwavering commitment to the cause.

"The sanctity of law can be maintained only so long as it is the expression of the will of the people." - Bhagat Singh
